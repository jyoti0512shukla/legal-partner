ARTICLE 1 — DEFINITIONS

"Confidential Information" means any information disclosed by one Party (the "Disclosing Party") to the other (the "Receiving Party"), whether orally, in writing, or electronically, that is designated as confidential or that reasonably ought to be understood as confidential given its nature and the circumstances of disclosure. Confidential Information does not include information that: (a) is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party; (b) was already known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure; (c) is independently developed by the Receiving Party without reference to the Confidential Information; or (d) is required to be disclosed by applicable law or court order, provided the Receiving Party gives prompt written notice and cooperates to seek a protective order.
