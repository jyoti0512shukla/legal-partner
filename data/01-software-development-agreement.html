ARTICLE 2 — SCOPE OF WORK AND SPECIFICATIONS

2.1 General Scope

The Developer shall design, develop, test, document, and deliver the Project in accordance with the Scope of Work and Specifications set forth in Schedule A. The Project shall comprise a comprehensive banking compliance software module that enables the Client to:

  • Monitor and ensure compliance with Reserve Bank of India circulars on digital lending, including the RBI Guidelines on Digital Lending of 2 September 2022;
  • Generate regulatory reports required under SEBI (Listing Obligations and Disclosure Requirements) Regulations, 2015;
  • Implement customer due diligence and anti-money laundering checks as mandated under the Prevention of Money Laundering Act, 2002 and rules thereunder;
  • Maintain audit trails and logs suitable for regulatory inspection;
  • Integrate with the Client's existing core banking and loan origination systems via approved APIs;
  • Provide role-based access control and segregation of duties in line with banking norms;
  • Support multi-tenancy for the Client's subsidiary and affiliate entities.

2.2 Phases and Timeline

The Project shall be executed in the following phases, each with specified deliverables and acceptance criteria:

Phase 1 — Discovery, Design & Architecture (28 February 2025): Preparation of detailed technical design document, architecture diagrams, database schema, API specifications, and project plan. Deliverables shall include: (i) Technical Architecture Document; (ii) Data Flow Diagrams; (iii) Entity Relationship Diagrams; (iv) API Specification; (v) Security Design Document; (vi) Detailed Project Schedule.

Phase 2 — Development & Unit Testing (30 April 2025): Development of all modules, including backend services, frontend interfaces, database objects, and integration layers. Developer shall conduct unit testing and provide weekly progress reports. Deliverables shall include: (i) Source code for all modules; (ii) Unit test cases and results; (iii) Developer documentation; (iv) Deployment scripts.

Phase 3 — Integration, UAT & Deployment (30 June 2025): Integration testing, user acceptance testing support, performance testing, security testing, and deployment to staging environment. Upon Acceptance, deployment to production. Deliverables shall include: (i) Integration test report; (ii) UAT support and defect resolution; (iii) Performance test report; (iv) Security audit report; (v) User manual and administrator guide; (vi) Production deployment.

2.3 Compliance Requirements

All Deliverables shall comply with: (a) applicable provisions of the Indian Contract Act, 1872; (b) the Information Technology Act, 2000 and the Information Technology (Reasonable Security Practices and Procedures and Sensitive Personal Data or Information) Rules, 2011; (c) RBI guidelines on technology risk management and cybersecurity; (d) SEBI cybersecurity framework where applicable; (e) data localization requirements under applicable law; (f) accessibility standards to the extent mandated.

2.4 Assumptions and Dependencies

The Developer's ability to perform the Services is contingent upon the following assumptions, which the Client represents to be true and shall ensure remain true: (a) the Client shall provide timely access to its systems, APIs, and personnel; (b) the Client shall provide accurate and complete requirements, business rules, and sample data; (c) the Client's existing systems (core banking, loan origination) shall have stable, documented APIs as represented; (d) the Client shall designate a single point of contact for requirements clarification and shall respond to queries within five (5) business days; (e) the Client shall provide a staging environment meeting the minimum specifications in Annexure VIII; (f) no material change in applicable regulatory requirements during the development period that would require fundamental redesign; (g) the Client shall obtain any third-party consents or licences required for integration. Failure to agree within fifteen (15) days may entitle the Developer to an extension of time or, if the impact is severe, to terminate for convenience.
