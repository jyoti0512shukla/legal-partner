JOINT VENTURE AGREEMENT

Agreement No: JVA/2025/001
Effective Date: 15 June 2025
Governing Law: Indian Contract Act, 1872; Companies Act, 2013; Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996

PARTIES

PARTY A: GreenEnergy Solutions Private Limited, CIN U40109MH2010PTC203456, having its registered office at 34, Bandra Kurla Complex, Mumbai, Maharashtra 400051 ("Party A").

PARTY B: SolarTech Infrastructure Private Limited, CIN U74999GJ2012PTC070123, having its registered office at 78, SG Highway, Ahmedabad, Gujarat 380054 ("Party B").

Party A and Party B are each a "Party" and collectively the "Parties".

RECITALS

WHEREAS the Parties wish to establish a joint venture company ("JV Company") for the development, construction, and operation of solar power projects in India;

WHEREAS Party A brings expertise in renewable energy project development and financing;

WHEREAS Party B brings expertise in solar technology and EPC capabilities;

NOW, THEREFORE, the Parties agree as follows:

ARTICLE 1 — DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION

1.1 Definitions

"Affiliate" means, with respect to any entity, any other entity that directly or indirectly controls, is controlled by, or is under common control with such entity. "Control" means ownership of more than fifty percent (50%) of the voting equity or the power to direct management and policies. "Board" means the Board of Directors of the JV Company. "Business" has the meaning in Article 2. "Business Day" means any day other than Saturday, Sunday, or a public holiday in Maharashtra and Gujarat. "Effective Date" means 15 June 2025. "JV Company" means the company to be incorporated by the Parties. "Reserved Matters" has the meaning in Schedule B.

1.2 Further Definitions

"Material" when used in respect of breach, change, or impact means significant enough to materially affect the value or operations of the JV Company or a Party's rights. "Representatives" means directors, officers, employees, and professional advisors. "Fair Value" means value determined in accordance with ICAI Valuation Standards. "Deadlock" means the Board's inability to pass a resolution on a Reserved Matter due to lack of requisite approval.

1.3 Interpretation

In this Agreement: (a) headings are for convenience only; (b) singular includes plural; (c) "include" and "including" are without limitation; (d) references to statutes include amendments and subordinate legislation; (e) "or" is not exclusive; (f) "days" means calendar days unless "business days" specified; (g) "writing" includes email where acknowledged; (h) in case of conflict between this Agreement and the JV Company's Articles of Association, this Agreement shall prevail as between the Parties, subject to mandatory law; (i) "knowledge" of a Party means the actual knowledge of its directors and officers after reasonable inquiry.

ARTICLE 2 — JV COMPANY

The Parties shall incorporate a private limited company under the Companies Act, 2013. Shareholding: Party A 51%; Party B 49%. The JV Company shall have an authorised capital of ₹10 Crore; initial issued capital as per the Subscription Agreement. Registered office: Gujarat. The JV Company shall be the vehicle for the Business.

ARTICLE 3 — BUSINESS

The Business comprises: (a) development of solar power projects (grid-connected, rooftop, hybrid); (b) EPC and O&M services for solar projects; (c) sale of power under PPAs; (d) related activities. Geographic focus: India. The JV Company shall not engage in activities outside the Business without unanimous Board approval.

ARTICLE 4 — GOVERNANCE

Board of five (5) Directors: Party A nominates three (3); Party B nominates two (2). Chairperson nominated by Party A. Quorum: at least one Director from each Party. Reserved matters requiring unanimous approval: change in Business; significant new business line; borrowing above ₹25 Crore; related party transactions; change in shareholding; liquidation. Other matters: simple majority.

ARTICLE 5 — CAPITAL AND FUNDING

Initial equity: as per Subscription Agreement. Subsequent funding: pro-rata by shareholding unless otherwise agreed. If a Party does not participate in a funding round, dilution as per standard valuation. Debt: as approved by the Board. Bank accounts: operations as per approved signatories.

ARTICLE 6 — TRANSFER OF SHARES

No transfer without the other Party's consent (except to Affiliate with same obligations). Right of first offer: selling Party must offer to the other at the same price. Tag-along: if one Party sells to a third party, the other may sell on same terms. Drag-along: if Parties holding 75% agree to sell the JV Company, the minority must sell on same terms.

ARTICLE 6 — NON-COMPETE AND EXCLUSIVITY

During the term and for two (2) years after exit, neither Party shall, without the other's consent, directly or through an Affiliate, engage in the Business in India in competition with the JV Company. Exceptions: existing business; less than 10% passive investment. For the first three (3) years, each Party shall route its India solar project development opportunities through the JV Company (subject to commercial viability).

ARTICLE 7 — TERM AND TERMINATION

Term: indefinite unless terminated. Termination events: (a) mutual agreement; (b) material breach not cured in ninety (90) days; (c) insolvency of a Party; (d) deadlock lasting twelve (12) months. Exit: put/call option at fair value as per ICAI valuation standards. Liquidation: as per Companies Act.

ARTICLE 8 — INDEMNIFICATION AND LIABILITY

Each Party shall indemnify the other for breach of this Agreement. Liability cap: ₹10 Crore per Party per annum, except for fraud or wilful misconduct. Force majeure: standard clause.

ARTICLE 9 — DISPUTE RESOLUTION

Negotiation for thirty (30) days. Then arbitration under Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996. Sole arbitrator; seat Mumbai; English. Courts at Mumbai for interim relief.

SCHEDULE A — BUSINESS PLAN (SUMMARY)

Year 1: Develop 50 MW pipeline; achieve financial close for 25 MW. Year 2: Commission 25 MW; add 50 MW pipeline. Year 3: Operational 75 MW; target 150 MW. Revenue model: PPA tariffs; EPC margin; O&M fees.

SCHEDULE B — RESERVED MATTERS

Amendment of JV Agreement; change in Business; borrowing >₹25 Cr; guarantee/security for third party; related party transactions; change in shareholding; appointment/removal of CEO/CFO; annual budget; material contracts >₹5 Cr; litigation settlement >₹1 Cr; liquidation.

ARTICLE 10 — DEFINITIONS

"Affiliate" means an entity that controls, is controlled by, or is under common control with a Party. "Business" means the solar power business as described in Article 2. "Board" means the Board of Directors of the JV Company. "JV Company" means the company to be incorporated. "Reserved Matters" means matters requiring unanimous Board approval per Schedule B.

ARTICLE 11 — INCORPORATION AND SUBSCRIPTION

The Parties shall cause the JV Company to be incorporated within sixty (60) days of the Effective Date. The Parties shall execute a Subscription Agreement for the initial share subscription. Authorised capital: ₹10 Crore. Issued capital: ₹5 Crore initially (Party A: ₹2.55 Crore; Party B: ₹2.45 Crore). The JV Company shall have a standard form Articles of Association as agreed. The Parties shall obtain necessary approvals (e.g., RBI if applicable, sectoral clearances).

ARTICLE 12 — MANAGEMENT

CEO: Appointed by the Board. Initial term: 3 years. Party B shall have the right to nominate the first CEO (subject to Board approval). CFO: Appointed by the Board. Party A may nominate (subject to Board approval). Other Key Managerial Personnel: As approved by the Board. Remuneration: As per Board policy and Companies Act. The JV Company shall have a registered office in Gujarat. Day-to-day operations shall be managed by the CEO under Board supervision.

ARTICLE 13 — FINANCIAL MATTERS

Accounts: Financial year: April–March. Accounts as per Companies Act and applicable accounting standards. Audit: Statutory audit by a firm appointed by the Board. Dividends: As declared by the Board, subject to profits and cash flow. Related Party Transactions: Per Companies Act; prior approval of Audit Committee and disinterested directors. Bank Accounts: Operated as per approved mandate. Signatories: CEO and CFO, or as approved.

ARTICLE 14 — DEADLOCK

If the Board is deadlocked on a Reserved Matter for more than sixty (60) days, either Party may invoke the deadlock procedure: (a) escalation to the Parties' CEOs for resolution; (b) if unresolved in thirty (30) days, either Party may offer to buy the other's shares at a price determined by an independent valuer (ICAI Valuation Standards); (c) the offeree may accept or counter-offer to buy the offeror's shares at the same price; (d) if both want to buy or both want to sell, the matter may be referred to arbitration for a buy-sell mechanism.

ARTICLE 15 — PRE-EMPTIVE RIGHTS

If the JV Company proposes to issue new equity, each Party shall have the right to subscribe in proportion to its shareholding to maintain its percentage. The Company shall give thirty (30) days' notice. If a Party does not subscribe, its holding shall be diluted. The Parties may agree to waive pre-emptive rights for strategic investors. Valuation for third-party issuance: as per ICAI Valuation Standards or arms-length negotiation.

ARTICLE 16 — NON-SOLICITATION

During the term and for twelve (12) months after a Party's exit, neither Party shall solicit the JV Company's key employees for employment, without the other Party's consent. "Key employees" means employees in senior or critical roles. General recruitment advertising is permitted.

ARTICLE 17 — CONFIDENTIALITY

Each Party shall keep confidential all information relating to the JV Company and the other Party disclosed in connection with this Agreement. Obligation survives for five (5) years. Exclusions: public information; prior knowledge; lawful disclosure. The JV Company's information shall be protected by both Parties.

ARTICLE 18 — FORCE MAJEURE

Neither Party liable for failure due to force majeure. Notice within 5 days. If continues beyond ninety (90) days, the Parties shall discuss alternative arrangements. If the JV Company is adversely affected, the Board may decide on mitigation (e.g., project delay, funding adjustment).

SCHEDULE D — PRE-EMPTIVE AND TRANSFER RESTRICTIONS

Transfer to Affiliate: Permitted if Affiliate assumes same obligations. Right of First Refusal: Selling Party must offer to the other at the same price and terms. Tag-along: If majority sells, minority may sell on same terms. Drag-along: If 75% agree to sell the JV Company, the minority must sell. Lock-up: No transfer for the first twelve (12) months except to Affiliate.

SCHEDULE E — COMPETITION RESTRICTIONS

During term: Neither Party shall compete with the JV Company in the Business in India. Exceptions: (a) existing business of a Party (to be carved out or disclosed); (b) passive investment <10% in a competitor; (c) with the other Party's consent. Exclusivity (first 3 years): Each Party shall route India solar project opportunities through the JV Company, subject to commercial viability and Board approval. Post-exit: Two (2) years from the date of exit. Geographic: India. Carve-out: If a Party exits and the JV Company ceases the Business, the restriction may be waived.

SCHEDULE F — EXIT MECHANISM

Put/Call: After year 5, either Party may trigger a put (sell) or call (buy) at fair value. Valuation: ICAI Valuation Standards, DCF or comparable transactions. Valuer: Mutually appointed; failing agreement, by the ICAI. Liquidation: If the JV Company is liquidated, net assets distributed per shareholding. Deadlock Exit: As per Article 14. Insolvency: If a Party is insolvent, the other may acquire its shares at fair value.

SCHEDULE G — REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

Each Party represents: (a) it is duly organised and has authority; (b) no conflict with other agreements; (c) it will provide funding as agreed; (d) it has disclosed material information. Each Party warrants compliance with applicable law including FEMA, Competition Act, and Companies Act.

SCHEDULE H — INDEMNITY PROCEDURE

Indemnified Party shall give prompt notice. Indemnifying Party may assume defence. No settlement without consent. Survival: 3 years from breach except for fundamental rep (no limit). Cap: ₹10 Crore per Party per annum except fraud.

Each Party shall bear its own costs of negotiation and documentation. Incorporation costs (registration, stamp duty) shall be borne by the JV Company or as agreed. The JV Company shall reimburse reasonable out-of-pocket expenses of Directors for attending Board meetings, as per policy.

ANNEXURE VI — PROJECT DEVELOPMENT PROCESS

The JV Company shall develop solar projects through the following stages: (a) Site identification and land acquisition; (b) Feasibility study and resource assessment; (c) Obtaining necessary permits (environment, grid connectivity, land conversion); (d) PPA negotiation or tariff discovery; (e) Financial close; (f) EPC contract award; (g) Construction; (h) Commissioning and O&M. Each project shall require Board approval at key stages. Party A shall lead on financing and regulatory; Party B shall lead on technology and EPC. Budgets and timelines per project shall be approved by the Board.

ANNEXURE VII — INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY AND TECHNOLOGY

Any IP developed by the JV Company shall belong to the JV Company. If either Party contributes existing IP to the JV, such contribution shall be documented with a licence or assignment. The JV Company may use Party B's solar technology under a licence on arm's length terms. The Parties shall not use the JV Company's IP for their own benefit outside the JV without consent. Upon liquidation, IP may be sold or licensed as part of the assets.

ANNEXURE VIII — CONFLICT OF INTEREST

Each Party shall disclose any actual or potential conflict of interest to the Board. A Director with a conflict shall recuse from the relevant discussion and vote. Related party transactions shall comply with the Companies Act and be approved by disinterested Directors. The Parties shall not compete with the JV Company during the term and for 2 years after exit, except as permitted. Each Party shall route opportunities in the Business through the JV for the first 3 years.

ANNEXURE IX — INSURANCE

The JV Company shall maintain appropriate insurance: (a) project-specific (construction all risk, third-party liability); (b) general liability; (c) D&O insurance for Directors; (d) cyber and data insurance if applicable. The Board shall approve insurance programmes. Each Party may require the JV to add it as additional insured where relevant.

ANNEXURE X — COMPLIANCE AND ANTI-BRIBERY

The JV Company and each Party shall comply with applicable law including the Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, the Prevention of Money Laundering Act, 2002, and the Companies Act, 2013. The JV Company shall adopt a code of conduct and anti-bribery policy. No bribe or undue advantage shall be offered or accepted. Breach may constitute material breach. The JV Company shall maintain accurate books and records.

ANNEXURE XI — PUT AND CALL OPTION

Upon the occurrence of a termination event (deadlock, material breach, insolvency), either Party may invoke the put/call procedure. The initiating Party may offer to sell (put) or offer to buy (call) at a price determined by an independent valuer using ICAI Valuation Standards (discounted cash flow or comparable transactions as appropriate). The other Party may accept or counter-offer. If the Parties cannot agree, the valuer's determination shall be binding. Payment: 25% on closing; 75% in 3 equal instalments over 12 months with interest at 12% per annum. The selling Party shall provide standard representations and warranties.

ANNEXURE XIII — FORCE MAJEURE

Neither Party shall be liable for failure to perform due to force majeure (act of God, war, pandemic, government order, natural disaster, change in law). The affected Party shall notify within 5 days. Performance suspended for the duration. If force majeure continues for 90 days, the Parties shall negotiate in good faith. If the JV Company cannot operate for 180 days due to force majeure, either Party may propose winding up.
