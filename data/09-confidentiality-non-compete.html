PARTIES

RECITALS

WHEREAS the Employee holds a key position and has access to the Employer's confidential information, trade secrets, and proprietary technology;

WHEREAS the Employer operates in a highly competitive sector and requires protection of its confidential information and business interests;

NOW, THEREFORE, in consideration of the Employee's continued employment, access to confidential information, and the compensation and benefits provided, the Parties agree as follows:

ARTICLE 1 — DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION

1.1 Definitions

"Confidential Information" has the meaning in Article 2. "Competitor" has the meaning in Schedule B. "Effective Date" means 1 June 2025. "Key Customer" and "Key Personnel" have the meanings in Schedule B. "Restricted Period" means twelve (12) months from the Termination Date. "Termination Date" means the last day of the Employee's employment with the Employer. "Trade Secret" means information that derives economic value from not being generally known and is subject to reasonable efforts to maintain secrecy, including formulae, processes, source code, and customer lists.

1.2 Interpretation

In this Agreement, unless the context otherwise requires: (a) headings are for convenience only and shall not affect interpretation; (b) the singular includes the plural and vice versa; (c) "include" and "including" are without limitation; (d) "or" is not exclusive; (e) "knowledge" of the Employee means the Employee's actual knowledge after reasonable reflection; (f) "material" and "materially" connote significance in the context of the Employer's business; (g) references to "Articles" and "Schedules" are to this Agreement; (h) "days" means calendar days unless "business days" is specified; (i) "writing" includes email where acknowledged; (j) the Employee's obligations shall be construed narrowly where Section 27 of the Indian Contract Act is invoked, but to the maximum enforceable extent permitted.

ARTICLE 2 — CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

"Confidential Information" means all non-public information of the Employer, including: technical data, source code, algorithms, designs, formulations, processes; business plans, strategies, financial data; customer and supplier lists; pricing; research and development; and the terms of this Agreement. Exclusions: publicly available; rightfully known prior to employment; independently developed; lawfully received from third party.

ARTICLE 2 — OBLIGATIONS

The Employee shall: (a) hold Confidential Information in strict confidence; (b) use only for the Employer's business; (c) not disclose to any third party; (d) return or destroy upon termination. Obligation survives for five (5) years (trade secrets indefinitely). The Employee shall not use Confidential Information for the benefit of any competitor or for the Employee's own benefit.

ARTICLE 3 — NON-COMPETE

During employment and for twelve (12) months after termination, the Employee shall not, without prior written consent: (a) engage in or support any business that competes with the Employer in the development or sale of medical devices, diagnostic equipment, or digital health solutions in India; (b) join a competitor in a role involving similar responsibilities; (c) solicit the Employer's customers or key personnel. The restriction is limited to the Employer's actual business lines as of the Termination Date.

ARTICLE 4 — NON-SOLICITATION

During employment and for twelve (12) months after termination, the Employee shall not solicit or induce any employee, consultant, or key customer of the Employer to leave or reduce business. General recruitment advertising is permitted.

ARTICLE 5 — INVENTIONS AND IP

Inventions, developments, and IP created by the Employee in the course of employment or relating to the Employer's business shall belong to the Employer. The Employee assigns all rights and shall assist in perfecting title. Prior inventions shall be disclosed in Schedule A.

ARTICLE 6 — CONSIDERATION

The Employee's salary, benefits, stock options (if any), and access to confidential information constitute consideration. Upon termination, the Employer may pay a lump sum (up to three (3) months' salary) in consideration for strict adherence to the non-compete during the restricted period, at the Employer's option.

ARTICLE 7 — TERMINATION

This Agreement terminates with employment. The confidentiality and restrictive covenant obligations survive as stated. If any provision is held unenforceable, it shall be read down to the maximum extent permitted.

ARTICLE 8 — GOVERNING LAW AND DISPUTES

Indian law. Disputes: arbitration under Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996. Seat: Mumbai. Courts at Mumbai for interim relief.

ARTICLE 9 — REMEDIES

The Employee acknowledges that breach of the restrictive covenants may cause irreparable harm. The Employer shall be entitled to injunctive relief without bond. The Employer may also claim damages. The restrictions are reasonable and necessary to protect the Employer's legitimate business interests. If any restriction is held overbroad, it shall be enforced to the maximum extent permitted. The Employee shall not challenge the reasonableness of the covenants as a defence to breach.

ARTICLE 10 — TERMINATION OF EMPLOYMENT

This Agreement is part of the employment relationship. Termination of employment (for any reason) does not terminate the confidentiality or restrictive covenant obligations. The Employee shall comply with the return/destruction obligations. The Employer may place the Employee on garden leave during notice; restrictive period runs concurrently. Payment of any consideration for the non-compete (e.g., lump sum) is at the Employer's option and does not affect the Employee's obligation to comply.

SCHEDULE B — DEFINITIONS

Competitor: Any entity that develops, manufactures, or sells medical devices, diagnostic equipment, or digital health solutions that compete with the Employer's products or services. Key Customer: Customer with which the Employee had material contact or responsibility in the 12 months prior to termination. Key Personnel: Employee or consultant in a technical, commercial, or leadership role. Termination Date: Last day of employment. Restricted Period: 12 months from Termination Date.

SCHEDULE C — SCOPE OF NON-COMPETE

Geographic: India. Restricted Activities: (a) Employment or consultancy with a Competitor in a similar role (CTO, technical lead, product development); (b) Founding or investing in a competing business (other than less than 5% passive); (c) Advising a Competitor on technology or strategy. Permitted: (a) Employment with non-competing employer (e.g., hospital, academia, different industry); (b) Teaching; (c) Government service; (d) General technology consulting not specific to medical devices. Carve-out: If the Employee's role at a new employer does not involve competing products, the Employer may provide a waiver in writing.

SCHEDULE D — INVENTIONS AND ASSIGNMENT

Scope: All inventions, improvements, designs, software, processes, and works created (a) within the scope of employment; (b) using Employer's resources; (c) relating to the Employer's business. Assignment: The Employee assigns all rights to the Employer. The Employee shall sign all documents to perfect title. Moral rights: The Employee waives moral rights to the extent permitted. Prior inventions: Disclosed in Schedule A; excluded from assignment. Use of prior inventions: If the Employer uses prior inventions, the Employer shall have a royalty-free licence. Disputes on scope: Arbitration.

SCHEDULE E — CONSIDERATION FOR RESTRICTIVE COVENANTS

The Employee acknowledges that the following constitute adequate consideration: (a) continued employment; (b) salary and benefits; (c) access to Confidential Information and training; (d) opportunity to work on strategic projects. Optional: Upon termination without cause, the Employer may pay a lump sum (up to 3 months' salary) in exchange for strict adherence to the non-compete. Such payment, if made, shall be in addition to any severance. The Employee's obligations exist regardless of whether such lump sum is paid.

SCHEDULE F — RETURN OF PROPERTY

Upon termination, the Employee shall return: (a) all documents, files, and materials (physical and electronic); (b) laptops, phones, access cards; (c) copies of Confidential Information. The Employee shall delete all copies from personal devices and certify deletion. The Employee shall not retain any work product. Failure to return may result in withholding of final pay and/or legal action.

SCHEDULE G — ENFORCEMENT

The Employer may enforce this Agreement through: (a) injunctive relief (courts may grant without bond given irreparable harm); (b) damages; (c) arbitration. The Employee consents to jurisdiction in Mumbai. If the Employee breaches, the restricted period may be extended by the period of breach. The Employee shall reimburse the Employer's reasonable costs of enforcement if the Employer prevails.

SCHEDULE H — SURVIVAL

Articles 1 (Confidential Information), 2 (Obligations), 5 (Inventions), and Schedules B through H survive termination indefinitely (for confidentiality) or for the stated period (for restrictive covenants). The Employee's obligations are binding on the Employee's heirs and assigns. The Employer may assign this Agreement to a successor.

ANNEXURE III — GOVERNING LAW

This Agreement is governed by the Indian Contract Act, 1872. The restrictive covenants are subject to Section 27 of the Indian Contract Act; to the extent any covenant is held invalid (e.g., as an unreasonable restraint of trade), it shall be read down to the maximum enforceable extent. The Parties agree that the covenants are necessary to protect the Employer's legitimate interest in its Confidential Information, customer relationships, and workforce.

ANNEXURE IV — REMEDIES CUMULATIVE

The Employer's remedies for breach are cumulative. The Employer may: (a) seek injunctive relief to restrain breach; (b) claim damages; (c) terminate employment (if breach during employment); (d) withhold payments (to the extent of claim). The Employer need not elect; may pursue all remedies. The Employee consents to the jurisdiction of courts at Mumbai for interim relief. The Employee shall not oppose injunction on the ground of adequacy of damages where confidential information or customer relationships are at stake.

ANNEXURE V — NOTICE OF NEW EMPLOYMENT

During the restricted period, the Employee shall, upon accepting new employment, notify the Employer of the identity of the new employer and the role. The Employer may verify that the new role does not breach the non-compete. The Employer shall keep such information confidential. The Employee's failure to notify may be considered in any enforcement proceeding. This does not create an obligation on the Employer to approve or object; the Employer retains all remedies.

ANNEXURE VI — SEVERABILITY

If any provision is held invalid, the remaining provisions remain in effect. The invalid provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary to make it enforceable. If a court holds the 12-month period too long, it may be reduced (e.g., to 6 months). If the geographic scope is held too broad, it may be limited (e.g., to states where the Employer operates). The Parties intend that the covenants be enforced to the maximum extent.

ANNEXURE VII — ASSIGNMENT

The Employer may assign this Agreement to a successor (merger, acquisition, divestiture). The Employee may not assign. The Employee's obligations are personal and binding on the Employee. In the event of the Employer's merger, the surviving entity shall assume the Employer's rights. The Employee shall execute such documents as may be required to confirm the successor's rights.

ANNEXURE VIII — ENTIRE AGREEMENT

This Agreement, with the employment letter and referenced policies, constitutes the entire agreement on confidentiality and restrictive covenants. No prior oral or written representations. Amendments in writing. No waiver unless in writing. The Employee acknowledges that no promise of continued employment, promotion, or bonus was made in exchange for signing, other than as stated herein.

SCHEDULE I — LEGITIMATE BUSINESS INTERESTS

The Employer has legitimate interests in: (a) its Confidential Information and trade secrets (technology, processes, customer data); (b) its customer relationships and goodwill; (c) its trained and stable workforce. The restrictive covenants are designed to protect these interests. The duration (12 months) and scope (India, competing business) are proportionate. The Employee's role (CTO) gives access to sensitive technical and strategic information justifying the restrictions.

SUPPLEMENTAL PROVISIONS

SP.1 Section 27 of Indian Contract Act

The Parties acknowledge that Section 27 of the Indian Contract Act, 1872 renders agreements in restraint of trade void to that extent. The Parties intend that the covenants be enforceable to the maximum extent permitted. The covenants are narrowly tailored: limited to 12 months, to India, to competing medical device/digital health business, and to the Employee personally. The consideration (employment, salary, access to confidential information) is adequate. If any court holds a covenant unenforceable, it shall be severed or modified to the maximum enforceable extent.

SP.2 Specific Relief Act

The Employer may seek specific performance and injunction under the Specific Relief Act, 1963 to enforce the confidentiality and restrictive covenants. The Employer may seek temporary and permanent injunction. The Employee consents to such relief. The Employer need not prove inadequacy of damages for breach of confidentiality or restrictive covenants. The Employee shall not contest the Employer's entitlement to injunctive relief on the ground of adequacy of damages.

SP.3 Arbitration

Disputes shall be referred to arbitration under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996. Sole arbitrator. Seat: Mumbai. Language: English. The tribunal may grant interim relief. The award shall be final and binding. Courts at Mumbai for enforcement and interim relief. The Employee may also pursue remedies under labour law (industrial dispute, labour court) for employment-related claims.

SP.4 Notices

Notices to Employer: Legal/HR, MedTech Innovations, 15 Powai, Mumbai 400076. Notices to Employee: last known address. Deemed given: 3 days after posting if registered post; upon transmission if email. Material notices (termination, breach) by registered post and email.

SP.5 Severability

If any provision is held invalid, the remainder continues. The invalid provision shall be modified to the minimum extent necessary. If the 12-month period is held too long, it may be reduced (e.g., to 6 months). If the geographic scope is held too broad, it may be limited. The Parties intend maximum enforceability.

SP.6 Consideration

The Employee acknowledges that the consideration for the restrictive covenants and confidentiality obligations includes: (a) employment and the salary and benefits thereunder; (b) access to Confidential Information and training; (c) opportunity to work on strategic projects; (d) career development. The Employer may, at its option, pay an additional lump sum upon termination in consideration for strict adherence during the restricted period. The Employee's obligations exist regardless of whether such additional payment is made.

ANNEXURE X — GARDEN LEAVE

During the notice period of termination, the Employer may place the Employee on garden leave. During garden leave: (a) the Employee shall remain employed and bound by all obligations; (b) the Employee shall not report to work or perform duties; (c) the Employer shall pay salary and benefits; (d) the restricted period under the non-compete runs concurrently with garden leave; (e) the Employee shall not engage in any competing activity or join a competitor. Garden leave does not reduce the Employee's entitlement to salary or leave. The Employer may require the Employee to remain contactable and to hand over duties as requested.

ANNEXURE XI — CONFLICT OF INTEREST

During employment, the Employee shall not: (a) hold a material interest in a competitor (other than less than 5% in a listed company); (b) serve as director, advisor, or consultant to a competitor; (c) engage in any activity that conflicts with the Employer's interests. The Employee shall disclose any actual or potential conflict to the Employer. The Employer may require the Employee to divest or resign. Failure to disclose may constitute breach. This obligation applies in addition to the post-employment restrictive covenants.

ANNEXURE XII — COMPLIANCE WITH EMPLOYER POLICIES

The Employee shall comply with the Employer's code of conduct, IT policy, and confidentiality policy. The Employee shall use the Employer's systems only for business purposes. The Employee shall not download or transfer Confidential Information to personal devices without approval. The Employer may monitor use of its systems in accordance with law. Breach of policies may constitute misconduct and grounds for termination. The restrictive covenants apply regardless of the manner of termination.

ANNEXURE XIII — TAX AND WITHHOLDING

Any lump sum payment made in consideration for the non-compete may be subject to tax withholding under the Income Tax Act, 1961. The Employer shall deduct TDS as applicable and provide Form 16. The Employee is responsible for their tax liability. The Employer does not provide tax advice. If the lump sum is structured as consideration for a separate covenant, it may be taxed differently; the Employee should seek independent advice.

ANNEXURE XIV — MODIFICATION OF RESTRICTIONS

If the Employee's role or the Employer's business changes materially, the Parties may agree to modify the restrictive covenants by written amendment. The Employer may waive certain restrictions in writing (e.g., if the Employee joins a non-competing division of a competitor). Any waiver must be in writing and signed. Waiver of one breach does not waive subsequent breaches.

ANNEXURE XV — BLUE PENCIL

The Parties agree that if a court or arbitrator holds any restrictive covenant overly broad in scope, duration, or geography, it shall be enforced to the maximum extent permitted ("blue pencil" doctrine). The court may modify (e.g., reduce 12 months to 6 months, or limit geography to specific states) rather than strike down entirely. The Employee shall not assert that the entire covenant is void; the Employee agrees that a modified version would have been accepted at the time of signing.

ANNEXURE XVI — COOPERATION IN LITIGATION

During and after employment, the Employee shall cooperate with the Employer in any investigation or litigation relating to the Employer's business, to the extent lawfully required. The Employer shall reimburse reasonable out-of-pocket expenses. Cooperation includes: providing information, testimony, and documents. The Employee shall not assist any party adverse to the Employer in any matter relating to the Employer's Confidential Information or business. This obligation survives termination.

ANNEXURE XVII — EXISTING OBLIGATIONS

The Employee represents that the Employee is not bound by any agreement (with a prior employer or otherwise) that would conflict with this Agreement or the Employee's duties to the Employer. The Employee has not brought and will not use any Confidential Information of a prior employer. If the Employee is subject to post-employment restrictions from a prior employer, the Employee has disclosed such to the Employer. The Employer's obligations under this Agreement are not contingent on the enforceability of the Employee's obligations to third parties.

ANNEXURE XVIII — AMENDMENT AND WAIVER

This Agreement may be amended only in writing signed by both Parties. No waiver of any provision shall be effective unless in writing. No waiver of any breach shall constitute a waiver of any subsequent breach. The Employer's failure to enforce any provision shall not constitute a waiver. The Employer may enforce selectively. The Employee shall not rely on any course of conduct as a waiver.

ANNEXURE XIX — COUNTERPARTS

This Agreement may be executed in counterparts. Electronic signatures and scanned copies shall be valid. Each counterpart shall constitute an original. The Parties may exchange signed copies by email. A composite copy may be prepared for filing. This Agreement is effective upon execution by both Parties.
