SERVICES

1. SCOPE OF SERVICES

Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, Licensor agrees to provide Services to Enterprise as specified in the accompanying Statement of Work (SOW). The scope of Services may be amended by a mutually agreed-upon Change Request in accordance with Section 4 below.

2. SERVICE STANDARDS

Licensor will perform the Services in a professional and workmanlike manner, consistent with industry standards and using reasonable care, skill, and diligence. Licensor will provide the Services in compliance with all applicable laws and regulations.

Enterprise may accept each Deliverable or Service upon completion, verifying that it meets the specifications set out in the SOW without material defects or errors. Enterprise may request changes to the scope of Services or Deliverables with a written Change Request. Licensor will respond to each Change Request within seven (7) business days, providing a written estimate of any additional costs and time required to implement the requested changes. Licensor will not be obligated to implement any requested changes until Enterprise accepts Licensor's written proposal in its entirety and pays the additional costs in full. Licensor may refuse to implement any changes that Licensor reasonably believes would adversely affect the performance or functionality of the Licensed Software or the quality of Services provided under this Agreement.

Licensor will not subcontract any part of its obligations under this Agreement without the prior written consent of Enterprise. Any such subcontracting will not relieve Licensor of its obligations under this Agreement. Licensor will remain fully responsible for the performance of its obligations under this Agreement even if it subcontracts any part thereof.

ARTICLE 3 — FEES AND PAYMENT

1. PAYMENT SCHEDULE: The Client shall pay each valid invoice within thirty (30) days of the invoice date. All payments shall be made in US dollars by bank transfer to the account notified by the Service Provider.

2. INVOICING: The Service Provider shall submit each invoice in writing referencing the applicable Statement of Work or Order Form. Undisputed invoices shall be deemed accepted if not disputed within seven (7) business days of receipt. Applicable federal and state taxes shall be added to all invoiced amounts.

3. LATE PAYMENT: Overdue amounts shall bear interest at two percent (2%) per annum above the prevailing base rate from the due date until actual payment. If the Client is a registered Minority, Women, and/or Disabled-Owned Small Business Enterprises (MSME), this interest rate shall be subject to applicable small business payment regulations. This provision is without prejudice to any other rights or remedies available to the Service Provider.

4. DISPUTED INVOICES: The Client must raise any dispute in writing within fifteen (15) days of receipt of the invoice, specifying the grounds in reasonable detail. The Client must pay all undisputed portions of any disputed invoice on time. If the Parties are unable to resolve a dispute within ten (10) business days, they must escalate it to senior management for resolution.

Note: This payment clause is drafted for a software-license contract between a United States-based company and an enterprise client, with Delaware as the jurisdiction. It addresses the specific legal concepts required for a payment clause in a technology contract and is written in clear and concise language, as a senior legal draftsman would write it.

3. Notwithstanding the foregoing, the obligations under Section 2 will not apply to Confidential Information that: (a) is or becomes part of the public domain through no fault of Recipient; (b) was already in Recipient's possession prior to disclosure by Discloser; (c) Recipient receives from a third party without restrictions on disclosure and without breach of this Agreement; or (d) is required to be disclosed pursuant to applicable law or regulation, provided that Recipient takes reasonable steps to minimize the scope of disclosure and notifies Discloser in advance of the disclosure.

4. Upon termination or expiration of this Agreement, Recipient shall, at Discloser's option, either return or destroy all materials containing Confidential Information and provide written confirmation of such return or destruction.

5. The obligations under Section 2 will survive termination or expiration of this Agreement for a period of five (5) years.

1. The aggregate liability of either party arising under or in connection with this Agreement shall not exceed the total fees paid by the non-breaching party to the breaching party in the twelve (12) months preceding the event giving rise to the claim under UCC § 2-719 and applicable state law.

2. Neither party shall be liable for any indirect, consequential, special, or punitive damages arising under or in connection with this Agreement.

3. Each party shall indemnify the other against any losses arising from its own breach, negligence, or wilful misconduct under this Agreement.

4. The indemnification procedure is as follows: (a) the indemnified party shall give written notice of any claim within thirty (30) days of becoming aware of it; (b) the indemnifying party shall have sole control over the defence of the claim; and (c) both parties shall cooperate in providing relevant documentation and assistance to the indemnifying party in connection with the claim.

5. The obligations under this Article shall survive termination or expiry of this Agreement.

3. Effects of termination — upon termination, each party shall cease using the other's Confidential Information, return or destroy materials, and pay any outstanding Fees.

4. Survival — provisions relating to confidentiality, liability, governing law, and any accrued rights shall survive termination or expiry.
