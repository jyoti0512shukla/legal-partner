ARTICLE 2 — ENGAGEMENT AND SCOPE OF SERVICES

2.1 Scope of Services

The Consultant shall provide the following advisory services:

  • Due Diligence: Support in financial, legal, and operational due diligence of Target Assets; preparation of due diligence findings report; identification of key risks and mitigants;
  • Valuation: Independent valuation analysis using acceptable methodologies (DCF, comparable transactions, asset-based); preparation of valuation report for board and lender presentation;
  • Transaction Structuring: Advice on acquisition structure (direct, SPV, consortium); tax efficiency; regulatory approvals required;
  • Regulatory Compliance: Advice on SEBI (Substantial Acquisition of Shares and Takeovers) Regulations, RBI norms on NBFC/AMC investments, IBC provisions, Competition Act, 2002, and Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999;
  • Documentation Support: Review of term sheets, shareholder agreements, and acquisition documents; drafting support for negotiation;
  • Liaison: Coordination with resolution professionals, lenders, and other stakeholders as reasonably requested.

2.2 Exclusions

The following are expressly excluded unless separately agreed in writing: (a) legal opinions or advice (Company shall engage legal counsel); (b) tax opinions (Company shall engage tax advisors); (c) audit or attestation services; (d) financing arrangement; (e) guarantees or commitments on behalf of the Company.

2.3 Advisory Nature

The Consultant's role is advisory only. All decisions regarding the Transaction, including whether to proceed, the structure, and the terms, shall be made solely by the Company. The Consultant shall not have authority to bind the Company.

ARTICLE 5 — CONFLICT OF INTEREST

5.1 Disclosure Obligation

The Consultant shall, before accepting the engagement and throughout the Term, disclose to the Company in writing any actual or potential Conflict of Interest, including but not limited to:

  • Current or recent (past 24 months) engagement with any seller, resolution professional, competing bidder, or Target Asset;
  • Ownership of equity or debt in any party to the Transaction or Target Asset;
  • Personal or familial relationship with key personnel of the Company, Target Asset, or competing bidders;
  • Any other circumstance that could reasonably be perceived as impairing objectivity or independence.

5.2 Consequences

If a Conflict of Interest is disclosed and the Company determines it is acceptable, the Consultant may continue with such safeguards as the Company may specify. If the Company determines the conflict is unacceptable, the Consultant shall either (a) eliminate the conflict to the Company's satisfaction, or (b) the Company may terminate without liability for Fees for unperformed work. Failure to disclose a material conflict shall constitute material breach.

5.3 No Adverse Engagement

During the Term and for twelve (12) months thereafter, the Consultant shall not, without prior written consent, advise or assist any competing bidder or party adverse to the Company's interest in respect of the same Target Assets. This restriction does not prohibit the Consultant from advising other clients on unrelated transactions.

5.4 Ongoing Disclosure

The Consultant shall, upon becoming aware of any new Conflict of Interest during the Term, disclose the same in writing to the Company within five (5) business days. The Company shall have fifteen (15) days to evaluate and respond. Pending such response, the Consultant shall take such steps as are reasonable to avoid the conflict affecting the Services.

5.5 Chinese Walls

Where the Consultant has other engagements that could create a conflict, it shall implement appropriate information barriers (Chinese walls) to ensure that personnel engaged on this assignment do not have access to confidential information from conflicting engagements, and vice versa. The Consultant shall confirm compliance upon Company request.
