MARKETING SERVICES AGREEMENT

Agreement No: MSA/2025/1123
Effective Date: 10 May 2025
Governing Law: Indian Contract Act, 1872; Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996

PARTIES

CLIENT: Wellness First Ayurveda Private Limited, CIN U74999MH2018PTC312345, having its registered office at 23, Andheri West, Mumbai, Maharashtra 400058 ("Client").

AGENCY: BrandBoost Digital Agency LLP, having its office at 56, Koramangala, Bangalore, Karnataka 560034 ("Agency").

RECITALS

WHEREAS the Client is engaged in the manufacture and sale of ayurvedic health and wellness products;

WHEREAS the Agency provides digital marketing, branding, and creative services;

NOW, THEREFORE, the Parties agree as follows:

ARTICLE 1 — DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION

1.1 Definitions

"Approval" means the Client's written or email approval of deliverables. "Campaign" means a marketing initiative with defined objectives, budget, and timeline. "Confidential Information" has the meaning in Article 5 and Schedule E. "Deliverables" means all work product created by the Agency under this Agreement. "Effective Date" means 10 May 2025. "Media Spend" means amounts paid for advertising placement (Google, Meta, etc.), exclusive of Agency fees. "Services" has the meaning in Section 1.2.

1.2 Interpretation

In this Agreement: (a) headings are for convenience only; (b) singular includes plural; (c) "include" and "including" are without limitation; (d) "days" means calendar days unless "business days" specified; (e) "writing" includes email where acknowledged; (f) "material" means significant in the context of the engagement; (g) "reasonable" means reasonable in the circumstances of a prudent person in the industry.

ARTICLE 2 — SERVICES

The Agency shall provide the marketing services described in Schedule A, including: (a) digital marketing strategy; (b) social media management; (c) content creation; (d) paid advertising (Google, Meta); (e) branding and creatives; (f) SEO; (g) analytics and reporting. Detailed scope as per Schedule A and approved campaign briefs.

ARTICLE 3 — TERM AND FEES

Term: twelve (12) months from Effective Date. Renewable by mutual agreement. Monthly retainer: ₹3,75,000 plus GST. Media spends (ad spend) are separate and as per approved budgets. Payment: monthly in advance. Late payment: 18% interest. Price revision: after initial term, on sixty (60) days' notice, max 10% increase.

ARTICLE 4 — APPROVALS AND DELIVERABLES

The Client shall provide timely approvals (within five (5) business days for standard items). Delays may affect campaign timelines. The Agency shall deliver as per Schedule A. Revisions: two (2) rounds included per deliverable; additional at ₹5,000 per hour.

ARTICLE 5 — INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

Upon full payment, ownership of custom creatives, designs, and content developed for the Client transfers to the Client. The Agency retains rights to its pre-existing IP, tools, and methodologies. The Agency may showcase work in its portfolio with Client consent (not to be unreasonably withheld).

ARTICLE 6 — CONFIDENTIALITY

Each Party shall keep the other's Confidential Information confidential. Obligation survives for three (3) years.

ARTICLE 7 — INDEMNIFICATION

The Agency shall indemnify the Client for claims arising from the Agency's breach, negligence, or IP infringement in deliverables. The Client shall indemnify the Agency for claims arising from Client-provided content, products, or instructions. Liability cap: fees paid in six (6) months preceding the claim.

ARTICLE 8 — TERMINATION

Either Party may terminate for material breach on thirty (30) days' notice to cure. Either Party may terminate for convenience on sixty (60) days' notice. Upon termination: Client shall pay for services rendered and non-cancellable commitments; Agency shall deliver work product and transfer materials.

ARTICLE 9 — GOVERNING LAW AND ARBITRATION

Indian law. Disputes: negotiation for thirty (30) days; arbitration under Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996. Seat: Bangalore. Courts at Bangalore for interim relief.

SCHEDULE A — SCOPE OF SERVICES

Social Media: Content calendar; posting on Facebook, Instagram, YouTube; community management; paid social campaigns. Content: Blog posts (4/month); product descriptions; ad copy; video scripts. Paid Ads: Google Ads, Meta Ads; management and optimisation; budget as approved. Branding: Logo refinement; brand guidelines; packaging design support. SEO: On-page optimisation; keyword strategy; monthly report. Reporting: Monthly analytics dashboard; campaign performance; recommendations.

SCHEDULE B — FEES AND BUDGET

Retainer: ₹3,75,000/month. Media spend: Client approval for each campaign; typically ₹2–5 Lakh/month for digital ads. Production (e.g., video): quoted separately. Retainer covers strategy, creative, management, reporting. Media spend is pass-through; Agency may add management fee of 15% on ad spend.

ARTICLE 10 — APPROVALS AND FEEDBACK

The Client shall designate an approver. Standard approval cycle: 5 business days. The Agency shall submit for approval: creative concepts; ad copy; campaign plans; budget proposals. The Client may request revisions; two rounds included. Additional revisions: ₹5,000/hour. Failure to respond within 10 business days may delay campaigns; the Agency is not liable for delays due to Client approval. The Client shall provide brand guidelines, product information, and access to assets.

ARTICLE 11 — MEDIA, THIRD-PARTY COSTS AND BUDGET

Media spend is in addition to retainer. The Client shall approve budget before campaigns launch. The Agency shall provide monthly media reports. The Agency may earn commission from media platforms; such commission shall be disclosed. Production costs (photography, video, influencers): quoted and approved before incurring. The Client shall pay third-party costs within 15 days of invoice. The Agency shall not incur costs above ₹50,000 without prior approval.

ARTICLE 12 — WARRANTIES, DISCLAIMERS AND NO GUARANTEE

The Agency warrants that: (a) it will perform Services with professional skill and care; (b) Deliverables will not infringe third-party IP (unless based on Client materials); (c) it has the right to perform. The Agency does not guarantee specific results (ROI, reach, conversions). Marketing outcomes depend on many factors. The Client is responsible for product quality and business decisions. EXCEPT AS STATED, SERVICES PROVIDED "AS IS".

ARTICLE 13 — LIMITATION OF LIABILITY AND CLAIM NOTICE

Except for fraud or wilful misconduct: (a) Agency's aggregate liability shall not exceed fees paid in the six (6) months preceding the claim; (b) neither Party liable for indirect, consequential, or punitive damages; (c) Agency not liable for Client's loss of profits or business. The Client shall notify any claim within thirty (30) days of discovery.

ARTICLE 14 — FORCE MAJEURE AND SUSPENSION

Neither Party liable for failure due to force majeure. Notice within 5 days. If continues beyond 45 days, either may terminate. The Client shall pay for services rendered and non-cancellable media commitments.

SCHEDULE C — DELIVERABLES TIMELINE

Content calendar: By 5th of each month. Social posts: As per calendar. Blog posts: 4 per month, weekly. Ad creatives: 5 days from brief approval. Campaign reports: Within 10 days of month end. Strategy document: Within 30 days of engagement. Revisions: 3–5 days per round.

SCHEDULE D — INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

Upon full payment, the Client owns: custom creatives, designs, and content produced for the Client. The Agency retains: pre-existing IP, tools, templates, methodologies. Portfolio: The Agency may showcase work with Client consent. The Client shall not use rejected concepts. The Agency shall not use Client's brand or materials for other clients.

SCHEDULE E — CONFIDENTIALITY

Each Party shall keep Confidential Information confidential. Use only for the purpose of this Agreement. Return or destroy upon termination. Survival: 3 years. Exclusions: public information; prior knowledge; independent development; lawful disclosure. The Agency may share with subcontractors on need-to-know basis subject to confidentiality.

SCHEDULE F — TERMINATION

For cause: 30 days' notice to cure. For convenience: 60 days' notice. Upon termination: (a) Client pays for services rendered and work-in-progress; (b) Client pays for non-cancellable media and production; (c) Agency delivers work product; (d) Agency transfers files and credentials. Early termination does not entitle refund of retainer for unused period.

SCHEDULE G — REPORTING

Monthly report: Campaign performance (impressions, clicks, conversions, spend); content published; recommendations. Format: PDF or shared dashboard. The Client may request ad-hoc reports; included if within scope, otherwise quoted. The Agency shall use industry-standard analytics (Google Analytics, Meta Insights, etc.). The Client shall provide access to relevant accounts.

SCHEDULE H — KEY CONTACTS

Client: Marketing Manager – [Name], [Email]. Approver for creatives and budget. Agency: Account Manager – [Name], [Email]. Primary point of contact. Escalation: Client CMO / Agency Director.

SCHEDULE I — ANNUAL REVIEW

At the end of the initial term, the Parties shall review: performance against objectives; scope; fees; renewal. Either Party may propose changes. Renewal on mutually agreed terms. If no agreement, the Agreement terminates; the Agency shall provide transition support for 30 days.

ANNEXURE I — CAMPAIGN BRIEF TEMPLATE

Objectives; target audience; budget; timeline; key messages; channels; success metrics; brand guidelines reference. The Client shall complete and approve before Agency commences.

ANNEXURE II — INVOICING

Retainer: Invoiced monthly in advance, due within 15 days. Media: Invoiced when paid to platforms, due within 15 days. Production: Invoiced on completion, due within 15 days. Late payment: 18% interest. Disputes: Notify within 7 days; pay undisputed amount.

ANNEXURE III — APPROVAL WORKFLOW

Step 1: Agency submits creative/brief for approval. Step 2: Client reviews within 5 business days. Step 3: Client approves, rejects, or requests revisions. Step 4: If revision, Agency revises and resubmits. Step 5: Maximum 2 rounds included; additional at extra cost. Step 6: Upon approval, Agency proceeds. Delay by Client beyond 10 days may affect campaign timeline; Agency not liable. For urgent campaigns, Client shall use best efforts to expedite approval.

ANNEXURE IV — MEDIA BUYING

The Agency shall place media buys as per approved budgets. The Client shall provide budget approval in writing (email acceptable). The Agency shall optimise campaigns for agreed KPIs (impressions, clicks, conversions, etc.). The Agency shall not exceed approved budget without consent. Monthly media reports shall show spend vs. budget. Any savings (under-spend) may be rolled over or returned per Client preference.

ANNEXURE V — THIRD-PARTY CONTENT

If the Agency uses stock images, music, or other third-party content, it shall obtain appropriate licences. The Client shall reimburse licence fees. For custom photography or video, the Agency shall ensure model releases and location permissions. The Client shall provide products and access for shoots. The Agency shall not be liable for third-party IP claims if it used properly licensed or Client-provided content.

ANNEXURE VI — TERMINATION PAYMENTS

On termination for convenience: Client pays (a) retainer for services rendered; (b) work-in-progress at fair value; (c) non-cancellable media and production commitments; (d) retainer for the notice period (60 days). On termination for cause: Defaulting party pays damages. No refund of retainer for unused period. The Agency shall deliver work product and transfer accounts within 15 days.

ANNEXURE VII — CREATIVE APPROVAL

All creative (ads, posts, designs) requires Client approval before publication. The Client shall not unreasonably withhold approval. The Agency shall incorporate feedback. If the Client requests changes that materially alter the concept, it may count as an additional revision round. The Client is responsible for final approval; the Agency is not liable for errors that passed approved content.

ANNEXURE VIII — BRAND GUIDELINES

The Client shall provide brand guidelines (logo usage, colours, fonts, tone of voice) at the start. The Agency shall adhere to guidelines unless agreed otherwise. The Agency may suggest improvements; the Client decides. Absence of guidelines: the Agency shall use reasonable judgment and seek approval for key elements.

ANNEXURE IX — REPORTING AND KPIs

Monthly report shall include: campaigns run; spend; performance metrics (impressions, reach, engagement, conversions, ROI where measurable); comparison to prior month; recommendations. KPIs to be agreed at campaign level. The Agency shall use industry-standard tools (Google Analytics, Meta Business Suite, etc.). The Client shall provide access to relevant accounts and data.

ANNEXURE X — CONFIDENTIALITY DETAIL

Confidential Information: business plans, financials, product information, customer data, marketing strategies, and the terms of this Agreement. Obligation: hold in confidence; use only for this engagement; not disclose except to personnel on need-to-know basis. Return or destroy on termination. Survival: 3 years. Exclusions: public information; prior knowledge; independent development. Agency may showcase work in portfolio with Client consent.

SUPPLEMENTAL PROVISIONS — INDIAN CONTRACT ACT

SP.1 Governing Law

This Agreement is governed by the Indian Contract Act, 1872. Formation, validity, and performance shall be construed under Indian law. Courts at Bangalore shall have jurisdiction. The Parties submit to such jurisdiction for all purposes including interim relief.

SP.2 Arbitration Procedure

Disputes shall be referred to arbitration under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996. Sole arbitrator mutually appointed; failing agreement within 15 days, by the Karnataka High Court. Seat: Bangalore. Language: English. The award shall be final and binding. The tribunal may grant interim relief. Costs as awarded.

SP.3 Limitation of Liability

The liability cap (fees paid in 6 months) applies to all claims. Neither Party liable for indirect, consequential, or punitive damages. The Client acknowledges that marketing outcomes cannot be guaranteed. The Agency's obligation is to perform with skill and care, not to achieve specific results. These limitations are an essential part of the bargain.

SP.4 Force Majeure

Force majeure includes: pandemic, government order, natural disaster, war, terrorism, embargo, strike, disruption of digital platforms (e.g., Meta, Google), or other event beyond reasonable control. The affected Party shall notify within 5 days. If continues beyond 45 days, either may terminate. The Client shall pay for services rendered and non-cancellable commitments.

SP.5 Entire Agreement

This Agreement supersedes all prior agreements and understandings. No amendment except in writing. No waiver except in writing. Severability. No third-party rights. Assignment: neither Party may assign without consent except to Affiliate or in M&A.

SP.6 Payment Terms

All amounts in Indian Rupees. GST extra. Invoicing as per Annexure II. Late payment: 18% per annum. The Client shall not withhold payment for disputed amounts unless it has given 7 days' written notice of the dispute. Undisputed amounts shall be paid on time. Disputes shall be resolved per the dispute resolution clause.

ANNEXURE XI — SOCIAL MEDIA POLICY

The Agency shall manage the Client's social media accounts in accordance with platform terms of service and applicable law including the Information Technology (Intermediary Guidelines and Digital Media Ethics Code) Rules, 2021. The Agency shall not post content that is defamatory, infringing, or in violation of advertising standards. The Client shall provide timely approval for posts. The Agency shall maintain editorial independence within brand guidelines. The Client retains ownership of account credentials; the Agency shall have access as necessary. Upon termination, the Agency shall hand over all accounts and access within 7 days. The Agency shall not use the Client's accounts for the Agency's own promotion without consent.

ANNEXURE XII — PERFORMANCE METRICS

KPIs shall be agreed at campaign level. Typical metrics: Impressions, Reach, Engagement Rate, Click-Through Rate (CTR), Conversion Rate, Cost Per Acquisition (CPA), Return on Ad Spend (ROAS). The Agency shall report against agreed KPIs. The Agency does not guarantee specific numerical targets; marketing outcomes depend on product, market, budget, and external factors. The Agency shall use best efforts and industry practices. If the Client provides conversion tracking (e.g., Google Analytics, Meta Pixel), the Agency shall implement and optimise accordingly. The Client is responsible for accurate tracking setup on its website.

ANNEXURE XIII — CONTENT OWNERSHIP AND LICENCES

Upon full payment, the Client owns: all custom creatives, designs, copy, and content produced specifically for the Client. The Agency grants a perpetual, royalty-free, worldwide licence to use, modify, and sublicense. For stock content (images, music) licensed by the Agency, the Client receives a sublicence for use in the approved context; the Client may need to obtain extended rights for other uses. For influencer content, rights depend on the influencer agreement; the Agency shall secure usage rights as agreed. The Agency retains rights to its pre-existing templates, methodologies, and tools. The Agency may use anonymised performance data for case studies with Client consent.

ANNEXURE XIV — CANCELLATION OF CAMPAIGNS

If the Client cancels a campaign after the Agency has commenced work: (a) for creative already produced, the Client shall pay 100% of the quoted production cost; (b) for media already booked and not cancellable, the Client shall pay the committed amount; (c) for cancellable media, the Agency shall use best efforts to cancel; any cancellation fees shall be passed to the Client. If the Client reduces budget mid-campaign, the Agency shall adjust; the Client shall pay for work done and non-cancellable commitments. The Agency shall not be liable for loss of anticipated results due to cancellation.

ANNEXURE XV — SUB-CONTRACTORS

The Agency may use sub-contractors (photographers, videographers, copywriters, etc.) for specific tasks. The Agency shall ensure sub-contractors are bound by confidentiality. The Agency remains fully liable for sub-contractors' performance. Key sub-contractors may be identified in Schedule A. The Client may object to a sub-contractor on reasonable grounds; the Agency shall use an alternative. The Agency shall obtain all necessary rights from sub-contractors (including work-for-hire or assignment) so that the Client receives clear title to deliverables.

ANNEXURE XVI — DATA PROTECTION

To the extent the Agency processes personal data on behalf of the Client (e.g., customer data for targeting, analytics), the Agency shall: (a) process only on documented instructions; (b) implement appropriate security measures; (c) ensure personnel are bound by confidentiality; (d) assist the Client in responding to data subject requests; (e) delete or return data upon termination. The Agency shall comply with the Information Technology (Reasonable Security Practices) Rules and applicable data protection requirements. The Client shall ensure it has lawful basis for any data shared with the Agency.

ANNEXURE XVII — COMPLAINTS AND ESCALATION

Complaints shall be raised in writing to the Account Manager. The Account Manager shall acknowledge within 24 hours and respond substantively within 5 business days. Unresolved complaints shall be escalated to the Agency Director. If the Client is dissatisfied with the Agency's response, the matter may be escalated to the steering committee or senior management. The Parties shall use good faith to resolve before invoking dispute resolution. Persistent failure to meet agreed standards may constitute material breach.

ANNEXURE XVIII — INSURANCE

The Agency shall maintain: (a) professional indemnity insurance with minimum coverage of ₹25,00,000; (b) cyber liability insurance if handling sensitive data; (c) general liability insurance. The Agency shall provide certificate of insurance upon request. The Client may require the Agency to add the Client as additional insured for relevant policies. The Agency shall notify the Client of any cancellation or material change in coverage.

ANNEXURE XIX — ANTI-BRIBERY

Each Party warrants compliance with the Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 and applicable anti-bribery laws. Neither Party shall offer or accept bribes, kickbacks, or undue advantages. The Agency shall not make payments to influencers or media that violate applicable law. The Agency shall maintain proper records of all payments. Breach may constitute material breach.
