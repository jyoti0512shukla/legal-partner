ARTICLE 1 — DEFINITIONS AND INTERPRETATION

1.1 Definitions

"Common Areas" means lobbies, corridors, elevators, stairwells, parking areas, and other areas of the Building designated for common use. "Building" means Tech Park Tower B. "Commencement Date" means 1 March 2025. "Lease Term" means thirty-six (36) months from Commencement Date. "Permitted Use" means general office, IT/software development, and related administrative use. "Rent" has the meaning in Article 3. "Security Deposit" has the meaning in Article 3. "Premises" means the demised premises described in Schedule A. "CAM" means Common Area Maintenance charges. "Rules" means the rules and regulations in Schedule D.

1.2 Interpretation

Headings are for convenience only. Singular includes plural. "Include" and "including" are without limitation. References to "Articles" and "Schedules" are to this Lease. "Days" means calendar days unless "business days" specified. "Writing" includes email. This Lease shall be construed in accordance with the Transfer of Property Act, 1882 and the Indian Contract Act, 1872. In case of conflict between the main body and Schedules, the main body shall prevail.

ARTICLE 2 — DEMISE, TERM, AND REGISTRATION

2.0 Stamp Duty and Registration

This Lease Deed is executed on non-judicial stamp paper of the appropriate value as per the Karnataka Stamp Act, 1957. The Parties shall cause this deed to be registered before the Sub-Registrar having jurisdiction over the Premises, in accordance with the Registration Act, 1908. The cost of stamp duty and registration shall be borne as per prevailing practice (typically by the Lessee). The Lease shall be effective from the Commencement Date irrespective of registration; however, registration is recommended for creation of a valid leasehold interest and priority against third parties.

2.1 Grant of Lease

The Lessor hereby demises and lets to the Lessee, and the Lessee hereby takes on lease, the Premises for the Lease Term, to hold unto the Lessee from the Commencement Date until 28 February 2028 (both dates inclusive), for the Permitted Use only.

2.2 Option to Renew

Subject to the Lessee being not in material breach, the Lessee shall have an option to renew for a further period of twenty-four (24) months on terms to be mutually agreed, exercisable by written notice given not less than six (6) months prior to the expiry of the Lease Term. The renewed rent shall not exceed 115% of the last payable rent, unless agreed otherwise.

2.3 Possession

Possession shall be delivered on the Commencement Date in the condition described in Schedule B. The Lessee acknowledges having inspected the Premises and accepted the same.

ARTICLE 3 — RENT AND DEPOSIT

3.1 Monthly Rent

The monthly rent is ₹4,25,000 (Rupees Four Lakh Twenty-Five Thousand) plus GST at 18%, payable in advance by the 5th day of each month. First month's rent shall be paid on or before the Commencement Date.

3.2 Security Deposit

The Lessee shall pay a refundable security deposit of ₹12,75,000 (Rupees Twelve Lakh Seventy-Five Thousand), being three (3) months' rent, prior to the Commencement Date. The deposit shall be interest-free and held by the Lessor. Upon vacation of the Premises in good condition and settlement of all dues, the deposit shall be refunded within thirty (30) days, less any amounts due to the Lessor.

3.3 Rent Escalation

Rent shall escalate by eight percent (8%) per annum, effective from each anniversary of the Commencement Date.

3.4 Common Area Maintenance (CAM)

CAM charges of ₹18 per square foot of carpet area per month (₹1,53,000 per month) shall be payable along with rent. CAM covers maintenance of common areas, elevators, security, and building management. CAM is subject to annual revision of up to ten percent (10%) upon sixty (60) days' notice.

3.5 Late Payment

Overdue amounts shall bear interest at eighteen percent (18%) per annum. If rent is unpaid for fifteen (15) days beyond the due date, the Lessor may exercise remedies under Article 12.

ARTICLE 4 — UTILITIES

4.1 Electricity

Electricity shall be supplied through the Building's common meter or sub-meter as per Schedule C. The Lessee shall pay electricity charges directly to the utility or building management as applicable. The Lessee shall not exceed the sanctioned load of 80 kVA without prior written consent. Additional load may require separate approval and payment.

4.2 Water

Water supply is included in CAM. The Lessee shall use water reasonably and report leaks promptly.

4.3 HVAC

Central air-conditioning shall be provided during Building hours (8:00 AM to 8:00 PM, Monday to Saturday). After-hours HVAC may be availed on prior request at ₹2,500 per hour, subject to availability.

4.4 Internet and Telecom

The Lessee may obtain its own internet and telecom connections at its cost. The Lessor shall provide reasonable access for installation. Cabling must comply with Building standards.

ARTICLE 5 — MAINTENANCE AND REPAIRS

5.1 Lessor's Obligations

The Lessor shall maintain and repair: (a) the structural elements of the Building including foundation, walls, roof, and load-bearing components; (b) exterior façade and common areas; (c) elevators, lobby, corridors, stairwells; (d) common building systems (electrical supply to meter, water supply, fire protection, building management system).

5.2 Lessee's Obligations

The Lessee shall maintain and repair at its cost: (a) the interior of the Premises including partitions, flooring, ceiling; (b) fit-outs, fixtures, furniture; (c) internal electrical and data cabling; (d) day-to-day cleaning within the Premises; (e) prompt reporting of defects requiring Lessor attention.

5.3 Damage

Damage caused by the Lessee or its invitees shall be repaired at the Lessee's cost. Damage from force majeure or Building defect shall be at the Lessor's cost (subject to insurance).

ARTICLE 6 — USE, ALTERATIONS, AND ACCESS

6.1 Permitted Use

The Premises shall be used solely for the Permitted Use. No retail, manufacturing, hazardous activity, or use that may increase insurance premium or violate law is permitted.

6.2 No Subletting

The Lessee shall not sublet, assign, or part with possession of the Premises or any part thereof without the prior written consent of the Lessor. Consent may be subject to conditions and payment of administrative fee not exceeding ₹50,000. The Lessee shall remain liable under this Lease notwithstanding any permitted assignment.

6.3 Alterations

Structural alterations are prohibited. Non-structural alterations, additions, or fit-outs require prior written consent and must comply with applicable law and Building rules. Consent shall not be unreasonably withheld for internal alterations that do not affect structure or services. At expiry, the Lessee shall restore the Premises to original condition unless the Lessor elects to retain alterations in writing.

6.4 Lessor's Access

The Lessor, its agents, and authorised personnel may enter the Premises upon reasonable notice (except in emergency) for inspection, repair, or showing to prospective lessees (during last six months of term). Access shall be during business hours unless agreed.

6.5 Lessee's Access

The Lessee shall have 24/7 access to the Premises via Building access controls. The Lessee shall comply with Building security procedures. The Lessor shall provide keys/access cards as required. Loss of access cards shall be reported immediately; replacement fee ₹500 per card.

ARTICLE 7 — RULES AND REGULATIONS

The Lessee shall comply with the Rules and Regulations set forth in Schedule D, and any reasonable amendments communicated in writing with thirty (30) days' notice. Material amendments shall not be imposed unilaterally; the Parties shall negotiate in good faith.

ARTICLE 8 — INSURANCE

8.1 Lessor's Insurance

The Lessor shall maintain: (a) fire and allied perils insurance for the Building structure; (b) such other insurance as is customary for commercial buildings. The Lessor shall use reasonable efforts to ensure the policy permits Lessee's occupancy.

8.2 Lessee's Insurance

The Lessee shall maintain throughout the Lease Term: (a) fire, theft, and plate glass insurance for its contents, fit-outs, and trade fixtures for full replacement value; (b) third-party liability insurance with minimum coverage of ₹1,00,00,000 per occurrence; (c) employers' liability if applicable. The Lessee shall add the Lessor as interested party and provide certificates annually. The Lessee shall not do anything that may vitiate or increase the premium of the Lessor's insurance.

8.3 Mutual Waiver

Each Party waives subrogation against the other for loss covered by insurance, to the extent permitted by the respective policies.

ARTICLE 9 — INDEMNITY

The Lessee shall indemnify and hold harmless the Lessor from and against any claims, damages, or expenses arising from: (a) the Lessee's use or occupancy of the Premises; (b) the Lessee's breach of this Lease; (c) acts or omissions of the Lessee, its employees, or invitees; (d) any alterations or fit-outs by the Lessee. The Lessor shall indemnify the Lessee for claims arising from the Lessor's default, defect in title, or defects in the Premises existing at Commencement Date (unless caused by Lessee).

ARTICLE 10 — COMPLIANCE

The Lessee shall: (a) comply with all applicable laws including fire safety, labour, and environmental regulations; (b) obtain and maintain all licences and permits required for its use; (c) not cause nuisance to other occupants; (d) comply with the terms of any superior lease or mortgage of which the Lessee has been given notice.

ARTICLE 11 — FORCE MAJEURE

If the Premises or Building are rendered unfit for use due to force majeure (fire, earthquake, flood, pandemic, act of government, war, terrorism), rent shall abate proportionately for the duration of such unfitness. If the Premises remain unfit for more than ninety (90) consecutive days, either Party may terminate this Lease by fifteen (15) days' written notice. Upon such termination, the Lessor shall refund the Security Deposit (less lawful deductions) and any prepaid rent for the period after termination. Neither Party shall be liable for performance delayed or prevented by force majeure.

ARTICLE 12 — TERMINATION AND FORFEITURE

12.1 Termination by Lessor

The Lessor may terminate: (a) for non-payment of rent or other dues beyond fifteen (15) days of due date; (b) for material breach after thirty (30) days' written notice to cure; (c) upon insolvency, winding-up, or dissolution of the Lessee; (d) for unauthorised use or assignment.

12.2 Termination by Lessee

The Lessee may terminate with three (3) months' prior written notice, subject to payment of early termination fee of three (3) months' rent. If the Lessor relets the Premises within sixty (60) days of vacation at the same or higher rent, the Lessor shall refund a proportionate amount of the early termination fee. The Security Deposit shall be applied against any dues; balance refunded within thirty (30) days.

12.3 Surrender

Upon expiry or termination, the Lessee shall deliver vacant possession in good condition (subject to fair wear and tear) and remove its belongings. Keys and access cards shall be returned. The Lessee shall not be liable for restoration if the Lessor has elected to retain alterations.

12.4 Holding Over

If the Lessee remains in possession after expiry without renewal, holding over rent shall be 150% of the last monthly rent for the first thirty (30) days and 200% thereafter. Holding over shall not constitute renewal.

ARTICLE 13 — DISPUTE RESOLUTION

Any dispute shall first be negotiated in good faith for thirty (30) days. Failing resolution, the dispute shall be referred to arbitration under the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996. Sole arbitrator mutually appointed; failing agreement, by the District Court at Bangalore. Seat: Bangalore. Language: English. The courts at Bangalore shall have exclusive jurisdiction for interim relief.

ARTICLE 14 — GENERAL

Entire agreement. Amendments in writing. Severability. No waiver unless in writing. Notices to registered address by registered post or email. Governing law: laws of India including Transfer of Property Act, 1882 and Indian Contract Act, 1872.
